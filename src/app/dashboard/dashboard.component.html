Introduction

The treatise “Shri Guru Bhagavat” (published in five volumes) originally written in Odia language is an unparalleled and supreme creation of Guruji Dr. Chandrabahnu Satpathy. This poetic treatise stands like a light house for every spiritual traveler and shows divine path towards knowledge, devotion and rightful action. This book elaborately depicts the theme of devotion towards the Guru ( The Spiritual master) with the basic features of Guru tradition. This treatise contains invaluable aspects of human life like the greatness and uniqueness of Shri Guru, the different level of Gurus, Guru-disciple relationship and the importance of rendering services to the Guru. Behind the creation of this epoch making Treatise, Dr. Satpathy had admitted that its due the blessings and inspiration of Sadguru Shri Shirdi Sai Baba this monumental task was completed. Guruji had compiled twenty thousand lines on five volumes of Shri Guru Guru Bhagavat written in poetic meter( each line with nine letters in couplet form) is a unique feature of this treatise. In true essence- Shri Guru Bhagavat is an encyclopedia containing fundamental principles and essential features of divine realm of the Sadgurus ( The perfect Masters) of the world. For every traveler in the spiritual journey, who aims to cross the ocean of worldly existence holding on to the boat of the Guru, this book is worth a treasure collection for daily reading. Until today, the five volumes of this treatise written in original Odia language have been translated into other languages including English, Hindi, Telugu and other regional languages.

The Bhagavat was originally written in Odia by Jagannath Das ( crown saint of Utkal) in the 15th century had influenced the main stream of Odisha’s life for centuries. The study of Bhagavat in places of religious congregations in villages, schools and every homes in Odisha had become indispensable part in the life of Odias for centuries. Odia Bhagavat had deeply influenced the spiritual consciousness, social consciousness and the educational and literary world of Odisha. Even today, Odia Bhagavat is recited during religious festivals and in the temples as well as in religious and literary congregations where it is frequently referred to. Being a part of Bhagavat culture with deep sense of spiritual knowledge, this Bhagavat movement was transcendental in bringing Shri Guru Bhagavat by Guruji Dr. Satpathyji in 21st century. To quote Guruji’s words – “ Many years ago, I had heard on of my near relatives singing some lines from Odia Bhagavat. I remember four lines of the Bhagavat which I recite mentally at times and derive utmost delight. The lines go like this-

❝

Govinda Govinda Govinda, Padu Khyaruchi Makaranda
Se Makaranda Paana Kari, Hele Tarile Brahanaaree

(This means “ Oh Govinda! From Your Holy feet, honey like nectar always flows. Drinking this honey nectar, the women of Vraj ( Gopi of the Indian epic called Mahabharat) got salvation”)

The beautiful and exotic tome in which she had sung these few lines had attracted me immensely and touched me to the quick. In the year 2001, I again had the chance of listening to her recital of these lines. This had illuminated my mind with exalted emotion, rhythm and imagination. One night around 11 p.m in the latter half of 2001 as I settled down to write a song on Sadguru Shri Sainath Maharaj, four lines in “Nabakshari Chhand” ( a poetic meter of nine letters in each line in a couplet) started flowing spontaneously in my mind in the same sweet tune as heard earlier. This is what came to my mind:

❝

Shri Gurudeba Gurudeba, Praana Mo Khoje Paada Taba
Tuma Shri Payara Dhyaanare, Ekaagrataa Aase Manare

This means “Oh Shri Gurudev (Oh the Master)! My soul ever yearns for Your feet. When I meditate on your feet, only then my mind gets absorbed in it”. While writing these four lines, I had entered into the plain of divine ecstasy and my entire night passed away in that state. Those four lines of the first day have taken the shape of “ Shri Guru Bhagavat” containing about twenty thousand lines”. As the thoughts started flowing spontaneously, Guruji went on writing this epic keeping Guru in his mind.
